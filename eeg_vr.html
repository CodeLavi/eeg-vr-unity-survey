Immersive EEG: Exploring Seizure Visualisation in VR with a Pico Headset

A hands-on study where you experience EEG seizure activity as immersive visuals and sound in VR (Pico headset, Unity app), and share your feedback through an in-headset questionnaire.

Overview

What this study is about

How can immersive VR help people see and hear EEG data in a way that is intuitive, learnable, and clinically useful? In this study, you will wear a Pico VR headset, experience 3D visualisations and sonifications of seizure-related EEG patterns, and complete a short questionnaire inside VR.

  • Session duration: ~45–60 minutes
  • Location: UCC (room TBA)
  • Format: Individual, guided session

Who can take part

We welcome:

  • Clinicians, EEG / neurology / epilepsy professionals
  • Students or researchers in medicine, neuroscience, biomedical engineering, HCI, or related fields
  • Participants comfortable wearing a VR headset for ~30 minutes

No prior VR experience is required; basic familiarity with EEG is helpful but optional.

Study flow (approx. 60 minutes)

  1. Welcome & consent (5–10 min)
    Introduction, consent form, brief background questionnaire.
  2. Onboarding to EEG & VR (5–10 min)
    Short explanation of the EEG data and the Pico headset.
  3. Immersive EEG experience (15–20 min)
    Explore multiple VR visualisation and/or sonification conditions.
  4. In-headset questionnaire (10–15 min)
    Rate clarity, comfort, usefulness, and workload inside VR.
  5. Debrief & discussion (10–15 min)
    Post-session survey and optional discussion.

FAQ

What will I be doing?

You will wear a Pico VR headset, explore EEG-based visualisations and soundscapes, answer questions inside VR, and complete a short feedback form afterwards.

Is the VR experience safe?

We follow standard VR safety guidelines. You can stop at any time if you feel discomfort, dizziness, or eye strain. We will ask about prior motion sickness or VR issues before we begin.

How is my data used?

With your consent, we will collect your questionnaire responses and simple interaction logs. Data will be anonymised or pseudonymised and stored securely according to UCC and ethics guidelines. We do not record any identifying EEG or clinical data from you.

Accessibility

If you have accessibility needs, please mention them in the sign-up form or via email. We will do our best to accommodate you.
